GolGappa/PaniPuri Recipe

Ingredients:-

  1. For the Puri:

    • 1 cup semolina (sooji or rava)
    • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour (maida)
    • A pinch of salt
    • Water for kneading
    • Oil for deep frying

  2. For the Pani (Spicy Water):

    • 1 cup mint leaves
    • ½ cup coriander leaves
    • 2-3 green chilies
    • 1-inch piece of ginger
    • 1 small onion
    • 1 tablespoon tamarind pulp
    • 1 teaspoon roasted cumin powder
    • 1 teaspoon chaat masala
    • 1 teaspoon black salt
    • Salt to taste
    • 4 cups chilled water

  3. For the Filling:

    • 1 cup boiled and mashed potatoes
    • ½ cup boiled chickpeas (optional)
    • ½ cup finely chopped onions
    • ½ cup finely chopped tomatoes
    • 2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves
    • 1 teaspoon chaat masala
    • Salt to taste

  4. For Sweet Tamarind Chutney:

    • ½ cup tamarind pulp
    • ½ cup jaggery or sugar
    • 1 teaspoon roasted cumin powder
    • A pinch of salt
    • Water as needed

  5. Instructions:

    • Puri Preparation:

      • In a mixing bowl, combine semolina, all-purpose flour, and a pinch of salt. Gradually add water and knead into a stiff dough. Cover it with a damp cloth and let it rest for 15-20 minutes. Divide the dough into small balls. Roll each ball into a thin circle. Heat oil in a deep frying pan. Fry the puris one by one until they puff up and turn golden brown. Remove and drain on a paper towel. Set aside.

    • Pani Preparation:

      • In a blender, add mint leaves, coriander leaves, green chilies, ginger, and onion. Blend until smooth. Transfer the mixture into a large bowl. Add tamarind pulp, roasted cumin powder, chaat masala, black salt, and regular salt. Mix well. Gradually add chilled water while stirring continuously. Adjust the seasoning according to your taste. Refrigerate the pani to enhance the flavors.

    • Filling Preparation:

      • In a bowl, mix together mashed potatoes, boiled chickpeas (if using), chopped onions, chopped tomatoes, coriander leaves, chaat masala, and salt. Mix well to combine all the ingredients. Set aside.

    • Sweet Tamarind Chutney:

      • In a saucepan, combine tamarind pulp, jaggery or sugar, roasted cumin powder, salt, and water. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the mixture thickens to a chutney-like consistency. Remove from heat and allow it to cool.

    • Assembling the Pani Puri:

      • Take a puri and gently make a small hole in the center using your thumb. Fill the puri with a spoonful of the prepared filling mixture. Add a little sweet tamarind chutney and dip the puri into the spicy pani. Pop the whole Pani Puri into your mouth and enjoy the burst of flavors. Serve the Pani Puri immediately and repeat the process for each puri. Remember to adjust the filling and spices according to your preference. Pani Puri is a delightful street food snack enjoyed by many, so savor each bite and enjoy the tangy, spicy, and sweet flavors all in one go!
